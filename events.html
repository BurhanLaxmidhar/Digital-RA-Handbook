Event Planning

Ideas and resources for building community in your hall.

Event Ideas

Event Name Description Estimated Cost Tips
Movie Night Screen a popular film in the lounge with popcorn and snacks. $15 Reserve the lounge in advance. Let residents vote on the movie.
Board Game Social Bring board games or cards for a fun, low-cost evening. $5 Borrow games from the front desk or residents.
Trivia Night Host a trivia contest on campus facts, pop culture, or themed topics. $10 Offer small prizes for winners. Teams encourage new connections.
DIY Craft Night Provide supplies for residents to make crafts or decorations. $20 Easy crafts: friendship bracelets, canvas painting, origami.
Wellness Walk Organize a group walk around campus or the neighborhood. $0 Combine with a scavenger hunt for extra fun.

How to Plan a Successful Event

  1. Check your hall’s event policy and budget guidelines.
  2. Pick a date/time that works for most residents (avoid midterms!)
  3. Reserve any needed spaces or equipment in advance.
  4. Advertise using flyers, email, or group chat—remind them twice!
  5. Prep supplies early and set up before residents arrive.
  6. Greet everyone and help introduce residents to each other.
  7. Take attendance if required. Get feedback for future events.

Pro Tips for Inclusive Events

  • Offer non-food activities for those with allergies or dietary restrictions.
  • Schedule at different times (evenings/weekends) for maximum turnout.
  • Partner with other RAs for joint events and bigger attendance.
  • Encourage all residents, including quieter or new students, to participate.
  • Ask for event ideas in advance—your best suggestions come from residents!
