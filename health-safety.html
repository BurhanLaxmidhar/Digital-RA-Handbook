Health & Safety

Checklists, tips, and best practices for keeping residents and the hall safe.

Health & Safety Checks

Typically completed each month. Notify residents atleast three days ahead of the checks as per policy.

  • Check smoke detectors: present and working
  • Look for any prohibited items (candles, hotplates, eletric skateboard/scooters etc.)
  • Check cleanliness (trash, spills, pests)
  • Ensure windows and exits are clear of obstructions
  • Check that outlets and power strips are not overloaded
  • Document any violations or safety concerns and talk to the residents after

Rounds (Night/Evening/Breaks)

  • Check for propped or unlocked exterior/interior doors
  • Look for leaks, flooding, or spills in common areas
  • Report broken lights, malfunctioning emergency equipment, or unsafe areas
  • Check for unattended items in hallways or lounges
  • Note policy violations (noise, alcohol, etc.)
  • Monitor community wellness: look for students in distress, check on isolated or ill residents

Incident Reporting

  • Report emergencies immediately to campus police or emergency contacts
  • Document all incidents (safety, maintenance, wellness) as Incident Reports (IRs)
  • For urgent facility issues (flooding, power outage), call facilities/maintenance
  • Communicate with REA on-call as soon as possible

Wellness & Proactive Safety

  • Check on residents who appear ill or withdrawn; offer resources and support
  • Encourage open doors during move-in for community building, but secure them after hours
  • Participate in fire and safety drills; know evacuation routes
  • Maintain confidentiality and professionalism in all situations
